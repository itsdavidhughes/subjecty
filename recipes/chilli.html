Chilli

This makes enough chilli for 3 people for 2 meals each. After eating it for dinner it makes a great lunch and the leftovers are versatile enough to be eaten with a baked potato, in tacos, wraps or on top of sweet potato chips.

Ingredients

  • Any type of cooking oil
  • 500grams of lean mince/quorn
  • 1 large onion
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 Red Pepper
  • Half a pack of chestnut mushrooms

  • Bar of dark chocolate

  • Beef stock

  • 2 tins of chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tin of mixed beans in chilli sauce

  • Tomato purée with chilli

  • 1 heaped tblsp Hot chilli mix/hot chilli powder

  • 1 tblsp Cumin
  • 1 heaped tblsp Smoked paprika

  • 1 tsp Dried chilli flakes

  • Mature cheddar cheese

  • Greek yogurt

Prep

Dice onion as small as you can

Roughly chop the garlic small

Roughly dice your red pepper

Cut mushrooms whatever way you like them

Dissolve beef stock in a mug of boiling water

Start cooking

Get a big pot with a lid and pour loads of oil in so that it more than covers the base. Turn the heat up medium to high.

The base

This is the most important part of the chilli. Once your oil heats up, chuck in your onions — they should start frying immediately.

Stir fry those bad boys for about 4 minutes or until they go translucent.

Toss in your garlic and stir fry for another 2-3 minutes until you can smell it.

Throw in all your spices and continue to stir fry. You should start to see and smell a lovely base for the overall chilli forming.

Turn the heat down to medium - just don't let the spices stick to the pot. That's all your flavour ffs. The base should look dark, red, oily and delicious.

If it's looking a bit dry or sticky, it's probably because the powdered spices are absorbing the oil or the heat is up too high. Just add a little more oil and keep stirring to manage the heat.

Add your mushrooms and diced pepper and fry for about 5 more minutes so they absorb some of that flavour.

Mince

If your base is looking good and not sticking — crumble in your meat/quorn and turn the heat up high.

If you're using beef, you want to brown/seal the mince so there are no more pink bits. You'll need to smash it up with a wooden spoon in parts probably - don't want any meatballs forming.

Once the meat is browned, pour in the beef stock and bring it to the boil.

Sauce

Keep the heat on high, then pour in your 2 tins of tomatoes and about 2 tablespoons of tomato purée.

Mix it all about and bring to the boil. It should start to look a bit like a chilli now.

Put the lid on, turn the heat down and simmer for 15 minutes. Check it halfway through and make sure it's not sticking to the pot.

Once the 15 minutes are up, add your 2 tins of beans and bring to the boil again.

Once boiling, melt in the chocolate and stir. The colour should get slightly darker at this point. Less like a bolognese — more like a Mexican chilli. Turn the heat down to medium.

Cook on medium with the lid off for a further 15 minutes until you can smell the chocolate cooking.

Let it rest

I know you're probably starving, but this is really important. Rest your chilli off the heat, with no lid on — for at least 15 minutes.

Toppings

Take your mind off how hungry you are by grating your cheese and getting your yogurt ready.

Once it's rested, put your chilli in a bowl, add a tablespoon of yogurt and a handful of cheese.
